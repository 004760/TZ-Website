What emotions first come to mind when you eat different color skittles?

Data Collection

We collected data by going around lunch and giving eighth grade students some skittles as well as asking them to take the survey on schoology. We got 138 resoponses!

Data Analysis

Data Analysis

We were very set on using pie graphs because we know that it was the most clear and consise way of seeing what people thought of each color skittle.
We saw that people tended to think sweet for red skittles around 33%, this was not a blowout though, happy was close behind at 30%.
These are some trends we found in yellow, except that sad, depressed won.
We thought this was due to the sour tast and sour seemed to be more of a negative taste.
These are the clear patterns we saw but the rest is more vague and hard to inerpret, so we will leave that up to viewer opinion and thought.

Visuals

We wanted to use pie charts as visuals to find what people thought about skittles in terms of their color.

Conclusion

Overall green and yellow skittles made people feel sad and depressed as the other colors did not.
We found that When people eat red skittles they first think sweet over savory,happy and sad or depressed.
When people eat orange skittles they feel happy. We were wondering why doesnt the skittles company put more red and orange because they tend to recieve a positive response
but after doing some extensive research we found that they try to have the same amount in each package almost every time.
This puzzled us at first, but then we remebered that what sets skittles apart is the variety. so wether you like certain colors or not you are always getting variety.
That is part of the fun.
So next time you dig into a bag of skittles you are going to get a lot of different feelings as well as the full taste of the rainbow
